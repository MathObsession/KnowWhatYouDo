The brain works as the body’s control center, using billions of neurons to send electrical and chemical signals that help us think, move, feel, and remember. Attention span is controlled mainly by the prefrontal cortex, which helps us focus, plan, and avoid distractions. In today’s world, frequent screen use, multitasking, lack of sleep, and stress can reduce attention span by overloading the brain. However, attention can be improved with healthy habits. Getting enough sleep helps the brain process information efficiently, while regular exercise increases blood flow and brain activity. Practicing mindfulness, reading, and concentrating on one task at a time can strengthen focus. Eating a balanced diet and taking short breaks during study sessions also prevent mental fatigue. By training the brain with consistent routines and fewer distractions, attention span can gradually improve and support better learning and performance.
